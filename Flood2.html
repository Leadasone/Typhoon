    BEFORE (KNOW THE HAZARDS IN YOUR AREA)

  • 1. Monitor the news for weather updates, warnings and advisories.
  • 2. Know the flood early warning and evacuation plan of the community.
  • 3. Participate in community flood preparedness actions and drills.
  • 4. Secure your home. Move essential furniture and items to the upper floor.
  • 5. Before evacuating, turn off all the main switches of electricity, water and LPG tanks.
  • 6. Put lives stock and pets in a safe area or designated evacuation sites for animals.
  • 7. When order is received, immediately evacuate to higher grounds and safer grounds.

    • DURING (STAY ON HIGHER GROUNDS)

  • 1. Stay indoors and stay tuned for latest news and weather updates.
  • 2. DO NOT touch electrical equipment if you are wet or standing floodwater.
  • 3. DO NOT go swimming or boating in swollen rivers.
  • 4. DO NOT cross streams when water level is already above the knee.
  • 5. DO NOT walk or dive through flooded areas.

    • AFTER (STAY ALERT AND KEEP SAFE)

  • 1. Leave the evacuation area only when authorities says that it is safe to return home.
  • 2. Report fallen trees and electric posts to proper authorities.
  • 3. Check for wet or submerged electrical outlets and appliances before turning on electricity.
  • 4. Check your house for possible damages and repair as necessary.
  • 5. Make sure that the food and water for drinking are not contaminated by flood water.
  • 6. Throw away rainwater in cans, pots and tires to prevent the breeding of mosquitos.
